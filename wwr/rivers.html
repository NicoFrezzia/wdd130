Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return winds the Salmon river. The river earned the nickname “River of No Return” because many who ventured here fell in love with its rugged beauty and stayed. Herds of elk and deer graze the hillsides, and sightings of bear and bighorn sheep are common. The river is lined with towering ponderosa pine that offer shade at campsites. Rapids range from class I-IV, and the water varies from fast and cold in the early summer to slower and warmer in the late summer, ideal for families with children.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Winding through canyons in central Utah, the Green River flows towards the Colorado River. This 84-mile trip runs from Sand Wash to Swaseys Rapid and boasts outstanding scenery, fascinating geological formations, and great whitewater. Rated Class II/III, Desolation Canyon is usually done over 6 days and has been recognized as a National Landmark due to its historical significance.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

This iconic canyon offers the ultimate river rafting experience. Combining world-class whitewater with breathtaking scenery, the Grand Canyon promises an unforgettable adventure. The river is filled with exhilarating rapids and side canyons containing ancient ruins. Rapids range from Class I to Class X, offering something for all levels of adventurers.
